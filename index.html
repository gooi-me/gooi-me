What is compostable

Fruit and Vegetable Scraps

All fruit and vegetable scraps are compostable.

Coffee Grounds and Tea Bags

Used coffee grounds and paper tea bags are great for composting and add valuable nitrogen.

Garden Waste

Green garden waste is ideal for composting, but avoid large branches or diseased plants.

Paper Products

Only uncoated paper and cardboard should be composted. Avoid glossy or colored paper.

Food Scraps (Non-dairy and Non-meat)

These items are compostable but avoid composting meat, dairy, and oily foods as they attract pests and decompose slowly.

Yard Trimmings

These items are compostable but may take longer to break down. Shredding them first helps.

Natural Fibers

Natural fibers like cotton and wool can be composted. Ensure they are not dyed or treated with chemicals.
